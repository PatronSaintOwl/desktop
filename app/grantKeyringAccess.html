Grant Standard Notes access to password storage

To store your password securely, Standard Notes needs to save and retrieve passwords on your password storage facility, or "Secret Service" (using libsecret).

Due to limitations of password storage access, Standard Notes will be granted access to all your entries. We will not read these values, but technically, we can. If you do not have a "Secret Service" (also sometimes referred to as your keyring) or you prefer to use local app storage instead to store your keys, you may do so. You can add an application passcode to the app to encrypt your local storage.

To grant Standard Notes access to your passwords, visit the Standard Notes page in your software store an look for a Permissions button. Then, quit the app and start it again.

Alternatively, you may run the following command in your terminal:
snap connect standard-notes:password-manager-service

Quit
